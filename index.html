Timeline of Life

  • Chris was born July 20, 1964
  • He attended Christ the King Catholic Elementary, where he was pulled out in 7th grade for being "too inquisitive"
  • At age nine, he found a large collection of beatles records abandoned in the basement of a neighbors house, and spent two years solid listening to them every day
  • Took piano and guitar lessons as a child, explaining that his mother saved his life by buying him a snare drum: starting his journey to becoming a Rock Musician
  • He described himself as a loner, finding the strength to cope with this through Rock music
  • At the age of 12, he had access to alcohol, marijuana, LSD, psilocybin, and prescription drugs, which he used daily until 14. He stopped for a year, but relapsed at 15
  • As a teenager, he suffered with deep depression, causing him to drop out of school and almost never leave his house
  • He credited Music for saving him during these times, however a bad PCP experience had him develop panic disorder and agoraphobia
  • Before becoming a musician, he worked as a busboy, a dishwasher, a fish seller at a seafood wholesaler, and a sous-chef at Ray's Boathouse in Seattle.
  • Was a member of a cover band in the early 1980's called The Shemps, featuring bassist Hiro Yamamoto
  • Soundgarden was formed in 1984 with Cornell originally on drums and vocals, bringing on a full-time drummer in 1985
  • Soundgarden signed to Sub Pop, and later SST Records, despite being "courted" by many other major labels
  • Released their first major album, Ultramega OK, earning a Grammy Award nomination for Best Metal Performance
  • Later, the band became the first grunge band to sign to a major label: A&M Records
  • After releasing Badmotorfinger in 1991, the band found itself with an unprecedented level of succes, along with other well known Seattle bands
  • "Rusty Cage" from this album was later covered by Johnny Cash on a 1996 album
  • Their fourth album, Superunknown, was their "breakthrough" album, debuting at number one on the Billboard 200, and cementing them as one of the most influential bands of all time
  • Tensions within the creative direction of the band caused it to disband on April 9, 1997
  • Cornell had many solo projects, including: Euphoria Morning(1999), Chris Cornell: Unplugged(2006), The Road We Choose - A Retrospective(2007), Carry On(2007), Scream(2009), Songbook(2011), Higher Truth(2015)
  • While recording the second solo album, he was in a motorcyle wreck, which launched him 20 feet in the air. He was not seriously injured, and returned to the studio later the same day
  • Audioslave, formed after Rage Against the Machine's singer left the band, comprised of RAtM members and Cornell on vocals. The band started in 2001
  • Their first album, Audioslave, was described as an amalgamation of the two bands, but by their second album, Out of Exile, they had found their own unique sound
  • On May 6, 2005, the band became the first American Rock group to perform in Cuba, playin a free show in Havana
  • Their third album, Revelations, was well recieved, but the band did not tour behind the release, due to their own projects
  • February 15, 2007, Cornell officially announced his departure from Audioslave. Soon after, the band officially disbanded
  • The band reunited after twelve years for the Anti-Inaugural Ball, protesting the inauguration of Donald Trump (Just another of many reasons to love Chris Cornell)
  • While still in Soundgarden, Cornell recorded an album with members of what would become Pearl Jam: Temple of the Dog. The album is a tribute to their mutual friend, and Cornell's former roommate, Andrew Wood
  • Wood, the former lead singer of Mother Love Bone, had died of a heroin overdose the year before
  • Around 12:15 a.m. on May 18, 2017, Cornell was found unconscious by his bodyguard in the bathroom of his hotel room, 1136, at the MGM Grand in Detroit, MI, after performing at a show with Soundgarden at the Fox Theatre on May 17
  • On June 2, 2017, the Wayne County Medical Examiner released its autopsy, concluding that "drugs did not contribute" to the cause of death
  • Cornell publicly talked about his struggle with depression, isolation and suicidal thoughts several times throughout his life
  • Following Cornell's death, his older brother, Peter Cornell, started a campaign to raise awareness on depression and suicide prevention.
  • Cornell's body was cremated on May 23, 2017. His funeral took place on May 26, 2017, at the Hollywood Forever Cemetery in Los Angeles. Seattle's Space Needle observation tower went dark from 9:00 p.m. to 10:00 p.m. PST on May 18, 2017, in honor of Cornell and his contributions to the city's music scene
  • A life-size bronze statue of Cornell was placed at Seattle Center in October 2018

For more info on Chris Cornell, and his amazing life, please visit his Wikipedia and his personal website.
